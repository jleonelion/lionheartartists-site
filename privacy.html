Privacy Policy

Effective: [DATE TO BE SET AT PUBLISH]

This Privacy Policy explains how LionHeart Artists ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects information you share with us, particularly through our representation inquiry and intake process at lionheartartists.com.

Because the performers we represent are minors (ages 4–18), most of the information we receive is submitted by a parent or legal guardian on behalf of a child. This policy describes how that information is handled.

Draft notice This document is a working draft prepared for the LionHeart Artists site. It should be reviewed by qualified legal counsel familiar with California privacy law (CCPA/CPRA), child performer regulations, and talent-agency recordkeeping requirements before being published.

1. What We Collect

We collect information that you provide directly to us through our intake form, email, phone calls, and any subsequent correspondence. This may include:

From the Parent or Legal Guardian

  • Name, email address, and phone number
  • Relationship to the child
  • General location (city / region)
  • How you heard about LionHeart Artists

About the Child Performer

  • First name and (optional) stage name
  • Date of birth and age
  • Gender expression for casting purposes
  • Physical details relevant to casting (e.g., height, hair and eye color)
  • Performance training, credits, and special skills
  • Prior representation history
  • Union status (e.g., SAG-AFTRA)
  • California work-permit status and Coogan account status (where applicable)
  • Publicly posted social-media handles and follower counts (when provided)
  • Goals, availability, and motivation for seeking representation

Submitted Media

  • Headshot and full-length photographs uploaded through the intake form
  • Links to externally hosted demo reels, self-tapes, or résumés (e.g., Vimeo, YouTube, Google Drive)

Automatically Collected

Our marketing site does not use analytics or advertising cookies. Our intake form uses Cloudflare Turnstile to prevent automated abuse; Turnstile may collect limited technical information (such as IP address and browser signals) solely to distinguish humans from bots. It is not used for tracking or advertising.

2. How We Use the Information

  • To evaluate whether a young performer may be a fit for representation by LionHeart Artists.
  • To communicate with the parent or guardian about the application, schedule meetings, and respond to questions.
  • To negotiate, document, and manage any representation agreement that may follow.
  • To maintain internal records of the agency's roster history and past applicants, which informs our long-term business operations.
  • To comply with legal obligations applicable to talent managers and employers of minors.

We do not sell or rent personal information, and we do not use applicant information for advertising or marketing to third parties.

3. How We Store and Protect Information

Information submitted through our intake form is transmitted over an encrypted (HTTPS) connection and stored within Google Workspace services (Google Drive, Google Sheets, Gmail) associated with the lionheartartists.com domain. Access is restricted to authorized LionHeart Artists personnel.

Uploaded photographs are stored in a shared team Drive and are automatically scanned by Google for malware before they are made available to our review team. We do not publish submitted photographs or share them outside the agency without the submitting parent or guardian's consent.

4. How Long We Keep Information

LionHeart Artists retains intake submissions and related records indefinitely as part of our long-term business records. This allows us to track the history of applicants and representation decisions over the life of the agency. A parent or guardian may request deletion of a specific record at any time (see "Your Rights" below); we will honor such requests except where retention is required by law.

5. Who We Share Information With

We share information only where necessary to operate the agency or where legally required:

  • Service providers: Google Workspace (for storage and email), Cloudflare (for form-abuse protection). These providers act as processors on our behalf and are bound by their own privacy terms.
  • Casting and industry partners: If a performer is signed, we may submit relevant materials (such as a headshot, résumé, or reel) to casting directors, producers, or other industry partners in the normal course of seeking work for the performer. We will discuss and confirm this with the parent or guardian prior to signing.
  • Legal or regulatory authorities: When required by law, subpoena, or other legal process, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of LionHeart Artists or others.

6. Children's Privacy

Our intake form is intended to be completed by a parent or legal guardian, not by a child. By submitting information about a minor, the submitter represents that they are the child's parent or legal guardian (or have written authorization from one) and consents, on the child's behalf, to the collection and use of that information as described in this policy.

We do not knowingly collect information directly from children under 13 through the public website. If we become aware that we have received such information without verified parental consent, we will delete it.

7. Your Rights

Parents and guardians who have submitted information to LionHeart Artists may:

  • Request a copy of the information we hold about your family
  • Correct or update any inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of a specific record (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Withdraw an application or representation inquiry at any time

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@lionheartartists.com from the email address originally used to submit information. We will respond within a reasonable time and, for California residents, within the periods required by the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the CPRA.

California Residents (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act, including the right to know what personal information we collect, the right to delete personal information (subject to exceptions), the right to correct inaccurate information, and the right not to be discriminated against for exercising these rights. We do not "sell" or "share" personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising as those terms are defined under California law.

8. Security

We use commercially reasonable safeguards to protect the information in our care, including encrypted transmission, access controls on our shared team Drive, and Google's built-in malware scanning for uploaded files. No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. If we become aware of a breach affecting your information, we will notify you as required by applicable law.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Effective" date at the top of this page. Material changes will be communicated to active applicants and represented performers via email.
